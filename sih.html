India faces a complex challenge in balancing its reliance on coal for energy with its climate change commitments.

Coal mining is a major source of carbon emissions, a greenhouse gas contributing to global warming.

To achieve carbon neutrality, the Indian coal sector needs to offset its emissions.

This can be done through a combination of strategies like reducing emissions from mining activities, adopting cleaner technologies, and offsetting remaining emissions by planting trees that absorb carbon dioxide.

A web-based application can be a powerful tool in this journey by helping quantify a mine's carbon footprint and evaluate potential pathways to carbon neutrality.
